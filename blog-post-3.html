Color Trends for Hawaiian Interiors This Year

By Aloha Color Painting Co. • May 8, 2025 • 6 min read

← Back to Blog

The Intersection of Island Culture and Global Design

Hawaiian interior design in 2025 is experiencing a beautiful fusion: the global influence of biophilic design, earthy minimalism, and grandmillennial warmth is meeting traditional Hawaiian aesthetics rooted in natural materials, ocean references, and the warmth of aloha.

The result is a rich palette that feels simultaneously contemporary and deeply rooted in place. Here's what we're seeing in Hawaii homes this year.

Trending Interior Color Palettes for 2025

Warm Terracotta & Lava Earth Tones

Inspired by Hawaii's volcanic landscape, warm terracotta, clay, and burnished copper tones are appearing in living rooms, dining rooms, and accent walls across Oahu. These earthy tones pair beautifully with natural rattan furniture, koa wood accents, and linen textiles.

Try Benjamin Moore "Earthen Jug" on a living room accent wall, or Sherwin-Williams "Spiced Cider" for a bedroom that feels grounded and warm.

Ocean-Inspired Blues and Aquas

From pale seafoam to deep ocean sapphire, ocean colors are always at home in Hawaii interiors. In 2025, the specific take is less literal — instead of cartoon-turquoise, we're seeing more sophisticated ocean blues like dusty teal, muted aquamarine, and deep coastal navy.

These work especially well in open-plan living areas where large windows frame views of trees or sky. The color palette connects the indoor space with outdoor Hawaii.

Organic Off-Whites and Warm Neutrals

Crisp, bright whites are making way for softer, warmer alternatives. Creamy white, warm linen, and pale blush are replacing sterile stark white in Hawaii homes. These tones feel more inviting in Hawaii's warm light and complement the natural wood and rattan furnishings popular on the islands.

Sherwin-Williams "Alabaster" remains one of our top-requested interior colors — it's warm enough to feel comfortable but clean enough to brighten a space.

Deep, Moody Accent Walls

Hawaii homeowners are embracing drama in individual rooms. Deep forest green, indigo, and charcoal accent walls are creating beautifully moody spaces, especially in home offices, primary bedrooms, and powder rooms.

Tips for Choosing Interior Colors in Hawaii

Remember that Hawaii's light — particularly the bright, diffuse light from large windows — makes colors appear lighter and more saturated than they look on sample chips. When in doubt, go one shade deeper than you think you want. Our color consultants can bring paint fans to your home and evaluate colors in your actual space, at no charge with any painting project.
