Exterior Painting Timeline: What to Expect from Start to Finish

By Aloha Color Painting Co. • March 28, 2025 • 6 min read

← Back to Blog

What Most Homeowners Don't Realize

The most common source of frustration we hear from homeowners who've had bad painting experiences is feeling surprised by the process. A project seemed to take longer than expected, or unexpected steps added cost, or the crew left before they thought the job was done. Understanding the full timeline of an exterior painting project eliminates these surprises.

Here is exactly what to expect when you hire Aloha Color Painting Co. for an exterior project.

Phase 1: Estimate & Planning (Days 1–7)

When you contact us, we schedule a free in-home estimate typically within 2-3 business days. At the estimate visit, Keola or a senior estimator will walk the entire exterior, note areas of concern (peeling paint, rot, failing caulk), measure all surfaces, and discuss your color goals.

We provide a written detailed estimate, typically within 24-48 hours of the visit. The estimate includes a breakdown of prep work, primers, number of coats, and the specific paint products we recommend. Once you approve, we schedule your project start date. During Hawaii's busy season (April–September), lead times can be 2-3 weeks.

Phase 2: Prep Day(s) (Project Days 1–2)

The crew arrives with pressure washing equipment. All surfaces are thoroughly cleaned, including soffit, fascia, and foundation areas. Landscaping and hardscape near the home are covered and protected. After washing, the crew scrapes loose paint, sands edges, applies mildewcide, replaces failed caulk, and primes bare spots.

For typical single-family homes, prep takes one to two full days. Larger homes or homes with significant deferred maintenance may require additional prep time — this is always disclosed upfront in the estimate.

Phase 3: Painting (Project Days 2–4)

Once prep is complete and the surface is fully dry (we always allow 24 hours after washing), painting begins. We typically apply the body color in two coats, with adequate dry time between coats per the manufacturer's recommendations. Trim, soffits, fascia, and doors are painted separately, often by a detail painter while the main body coat dries.

Phase 4: Detail & Touch-Up (Final Day)

The final phase is a complete walk-around with the homeowner. We check every surface, touch up any holidays (missed spots), ensure all caulk lines are clean, and remove all masking and protection. We don't consider a job done until you're satisfied.

After the Project

You'll receive care instructions, paint codes for future touch-ups, and information about your 3-year workmanship warranty. We also remain available for questions — we want every homeowner we work with to be a customer for life.
