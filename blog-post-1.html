Best Exterior Paint Colors for Hawaii Homes in 2025

By Aloha Color Painting Co. • June 2, 2025 • 6 min read

← Back to Blog

Why Color Matters More in Hawaii

When it comes to exterior paint colors for Hawaii homes, the stakes are higher than on the mainland. Hawaii's intense UV radiation, salt air, and tropical rain don't just affect paint durability — they transform how colors look on your home. A color that appears muted on a sample chip can explode with richness in Oahu's high-intensity sunlight.

In 2025, homeowners across Pearl City and greater Oahu are embracing a mix of classic island tones and bold contemporary choices. Here's what we're seeing on our job sites.

Top Exterior Color Trends for 2025

1. Warm Sand & Coral Tones

Neutral sandstone and soft coral shades remain hugely popular for their ability to complement Hawaii's red volcanic soil and lush greenery. Benjamin Moore's "Soft Pumpkin" and Sherwin-Williams "Coral Clay" are two of our most requested colors for single-family homes this year.

2. Ocean-Inspired Blues

Deep ocean blues and turquoise greens are seeing a revival. These colors work beautifully against white trim and feel right at home in a coastal environment. "Tropical Rain Forest" by Behr and "Outermost" by Benjamin Moore are standouts. For a more sophisticated look, consider a deep indigo like "Midnight Blue" on the main body with bright white fascia boards.

3. Charcoal & Modern Dark Tones

Contemporary Hawaii homeowners are increasingly drawn to dark charcoal, deep forest green, and near-black exteriors. These bold choices read as modern and luxurious against Hawaii's landscape. The key: pair with crisp white trim and natural wood accents on the lanai for contrast.

4. Classic White with Bold Accents

You can never go wrong with a clean bright white body (we recommend a warm white rather than stark white in Hawaii's light) paired with bold front door and shutter colors. This year, terracotta, deep teal, and mustard yellow doors are trending across Oahu's neighborhoods.

What to Consider Before Choosing

Before falling in love with a color, there are practical considerations. HOA rules, neighboring home colors, roof color coordination, and even your landscaping palette all play a role in the final result. Our color consultants can help you navigate all of this — it's included free with every exterior project.

Also consider sheen: for exterior use in Hawaii's wet climate, we typically recommend a satin or eggshell finish. These provide better moisture resistance than flat finishes while still reducing glare better than semi-gloss.

The Smart Approach: Sample First

Always test your top two or three colors directly on the exterior wall in large sample patches (at least 12" × 12"). Observe them at different times of day — morning light in Hawaii is very different from afternoon sun. Our team can apply sample patches during the estimate visit.

The best exterior paint colors for Hawaii homes are ones that feel authentically island while performing against Hawaii's demanding climate. We'd love to help you find yours.
